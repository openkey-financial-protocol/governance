# 🗝️ OPENKEY NETWORK

## Governance Constitution

**v2.1 - Cellular Architecture**

---

## 1. Mission and Vision

### 1.1. Mission
OpenKey Network is a cellular financial infrastructure that enables banking and exchange services (P2P) without centralized intermediaries, without censorship, and with privacy by design. Our goal is to empower each individual to be the sole custodian of their financial assets.

### 1.2. Vision
We envision a future where access to banking is an inalienable human right. OpenKey aspires to be the invisible infrastructure layer that allows sovereign transactions, operating as a **Symbiotic Agent** on the user's device.

---

## 2. Fundamental Values

### 2.1. Sovereignty and Immunity
The user is the sole owner of their keys. The cellular architecture distributes infrastructure across multiple independent "organs" (nodes, storage, messaging) to eliminate single points of failure and resist geographic blocking.

### 2.2. $0 Operating Cost
OpenKey commits to exclusively utilizing free-tier services and sustainable decentralized resources, ensuring the protocol's maintenance cost remains $0, democratizing access in restricted economies.

---

## 3. v2.1 Operational Paradigm

### "Open Core, Private Plumbing"

To ensure the project's survival against state actors or targeted censorship, OpenKey adopts a dual structure:

-   **Open Core:** The cryptographic engine, OpenFang logic, and smart contracts are public (MIT License) for auditing and trust.
-   **Private Plumbing:** Server configurations, proxy routes, API keys, and deployment scripts are kept private. This prevents adversaries from mapping the infrastructure for denial-of-service attacks.

---

## 4. Governance Model

OpenKey operates as an **Unincorporated Non-Profit Association (UNA)**. It is a voluntary agreement between developers and users.

### 4.1. Decision Making (OKIPs)
Technical updates are proposed through *OpenKey Improvement Proposals*. The central maintainer team coordinates execution, prioritizing technical resilience over commercial expansion.

---

## 5. License and Contribution

The codebase is distributed under the MIT license. Contributions are welcome as long as they align with the zero-cost and censorship-resistance vision. The creation of "Forks" is encouraged to ensure the protocol survives even if the main node is targeted.

> **Note on Forks:** Forks inherit the "engine" (Open Core) but must build their own "plumbing" (connection infrastructure).
